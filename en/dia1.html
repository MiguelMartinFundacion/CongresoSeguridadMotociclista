Deputy Director, APRSO

Eric de Seynes


IMMA President

In May 2023, Mr de Seynes, a French national, was elected President of the IMMA, the voice of the global motorcycle manufacturing industry. IMMA currently celebrates its 75th anniversary.

Mr Eric de Seynes also holds the position as President of the BoD of Yamaha Motor Europe and Senior Executive Officer of Yamaha Motor Co in Japan.

For the last forty years, Eric has always been involved in Motorcycle field since he has been starting his professional life, and even before. In fact, he has been racing himself in road racing, enduro, endurance and even Dakar rally since he was seventeen years old.

He has developed a very wide expertise and network of contacts related to PTW by his various experience related to product development, sales and marketing, motorsport and industry.

Between 2010 and 2016 he has been president of CSIAM, French associaton of vehicle manufacturers, director of ACEM since 2014 and more recently VP of ACEM, the European Association of Motorcycle Manufacturers. Mr de Seynes has also been leading various ventures and holds diverse board positions among which Chairman-Supervisory Board of Hermès International SCA.


Eric de Seynes

IMMA President

Ángel López


General Mobility Coordinator, Barcelona

Mr. Ángel López Rodríguez holds a PhD in Civil Engineering from the Polytechnic University of Barcelona. He is currently General Coordinator of Mobility at Barcelona City Council. Previously, he was Director of Mobility Services at Barcelona City Council from 2001-2012. In the past, he had management responsibilities in private high-tech companies. Pere Navarro


Director, DGT

Nació en Barcelona en 1952.

Ingeniero industrial por la Universidad Politécnica de Barcelona. Máster en Administración de Empresas en la Escuela de Administración de Empresas de Barcelona.

En 1977 ingresó como funcionario en el Cuerpo Superior de Inspectores de Trabajo y de Seguridad Social. Entre 1979 y 1983, fue Delegado en Girona de la Consejería de Trabajo del Generalitat de Cataluña. De 1983 a 1985, Jefe de Gabinete del Gobernador Civil de Barcelona. Entre 1985 y 1996, fue nombrado Gobernador Civil de Girona. Entre 1999 y 2004, Navarro trabajó en el Ayuntamiento de Barcelona como director de Transportes y Circulación y Comisionado de Movilidad. En 2004 es nombrado director general de Tráfico, función que desempeña durante ocho años hasta 2012. Entre 2012 y 2016 ejerció como consejero de Empleo y Seguridad Social en la Embajada de España en Marruecos. De 2016 a 2018, trabajó como Consultor de Seguridad Vial del Banco Mundial y la Corporación Andina de Fomento. El 29 de junio de 2018 es nombrado Director General de Tráfico.

Roy Rojas


Road Safety Council, Costa Rica, OISEVI

MSc. Roy Rojas Vargas, Senior International Advisor, has 30 years of experience in Mobility and Road Safety Policy Management in Costa Rica and the Latin American and Caribbean Region. He was Executive Director of the Road Safety Council of Costa Rica, and also served for 6 years as International Road Safety Advisor to the World Health Organization/Pan American Health Organization for Mexico and the Latin American and Caribbean Region. He is coordinator of the Ibero-American Commission on the Situation of Motorcycles and Road Safety of the Ibero-American Road Safety Programme/OISEVI. Swedish National Road Consulting AB awarded him an Expert Certificate for the Management of High Level International Technical Cooperation Projects in Road Safety, Stockholm, Sweden. 1999-2004. From 1999 to 2007 he was Technical Manager of the Technical Cooperation Agreement on Road Safety between Sweden and Costa Rica. In his country he coordinates the National Motorcycle Plan and is currently the Technical Manager of the Road Safety Council.

María Seguí-Gómez


Production Coordinator Global Status Report 2023, WHO

Dr. María Seguí-Gómez has a degree in Medicine and General Surgery (1991) and a Masters in Public Health (1993) from the University of Barcelona, as well as a Masters in Science (1995) and a PhD in Science (1999) from Harvard University. Since 1993 her work has focused on the study of the epidemiology of injuries, especially road traffic injuries, as well as the effectiveness and efficiency of measures to prevent them. Her time at Harvard culminated as Deputy Director of the Harvard Injury Control Center. She then moved to Johns Hopkins University where she joined the staff of the Injury Control and Research Center where she is still a visiting professor in the Department of Health Policy and Management. She later became a full professor (and later accredited as a professor) at the University of Navarra where she worked until her appointment as Director General of Public Health, Drug Addiction and Consumption for the regional government of Castilla La Mancha (2011) and Director General of Traffic for the whole of Spain (2012-2016). Under her leadership, traffic fatalities were reduced by 18%. She is a visiting professor in the Department of Emergency Medicine at the University of Virginia School of Medicine and Comillas Pontifical University. Dr. Segui currently works as an international consultant, among others for the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile, the World Bank and the World Health Organization where she coordinated the production of the Global Road Safety Report 2023. Her research led her to found and direct the European Center for Injury Prevention (2004-2011) which was recognised as a WHO Collaborating Centre. Her areas of special interest are injury information systems (development and quality), indicators and the effectiveness and efficiency of prevention and control measures. Dr Segui has given numerous lectures and courses, including the WHO Teach VIP course. She has been an editorial member of several scientific journals and participated in senior positions in different international professional associations including APHA, AAAM, or ISVIP. He has received several international awards.


María Seguí-Gómez

Production Coordinator Global Status Report 2023, WHO

Valentino Sevino


Director General and Interim Mobility Planning Area Director, Milano

Valentino Sevino is the General Director of AMAT, i.e. Agenzia Mobilità Ambiente e Territorio (Agency for Mobility Environment and Territory), a consulting company, totally owned by the City of Milan, committed to providing strategic planning and technical studies for urban mobility and environment quality. Young Tae Kim


Secretary General, ITF

Dr. Young Tae Kim is the Secretary-General of the International Transport Forum (ITF) at the OECD. Elected by transport ministers of the ITF member countries in 2017, he was confirmed for a second five-year mandate in 2022. Under the leadership of Secretary-General Kim, the ITF has grown from 59 to 66 member countries and into a truly global organisation with a presence on five continents. He has strengthened the ITF’s role as a convener of transport policy leaders and global platform for dialogue for better transport, inter alia by establishing Regional High-level Dialogues on Transport for Asia, Latin America and in Europe, that complement the ITF Summit, the world’s premier transport policy event and largest gathering of transport ministers.
Before becoming ITF Secretary-General, Dr Kim distinguished himself in the civil service of his native Korea, serving as Director-General in the Ministry of Land, Infrastructure and Transport (MOLIT). As Deputy Director-General from 2015, he was responsible for co-ordinating various transport policies including for autonomous vehicles, greenhouse gas reduction, urban transport, Intelligent Transport Systems and road safety, among others.
Dr Kim also held several Deputy Director and Director positions with responsibility for housing welfare, integrated city development and overseas infrastructure construction. He was seconded to the Prime Minister's Commission on Administrative Reform in 1996 and the Presidential Committee on Social Inclusion in 2005-07. From 2010 to 2014, Dr Kim worked at the Korean Embassy in Washington, D.C., as Counsellor for Construction, Transport and Maritime Affairs.
Dr Kim earned Master's degrees in Public Policy from Seoul National University, Korea, and in Urban Studies from Paris University de Vincennes-Saint-Denis, France. He also received his Doctoral degree in Political Sociology and Public Policy from the Institut d'Etudes Politiques (Sciences-Po) in Paris, France.
Dr Kim speaks fluent English, French and some Spanish, in addition to his native Korean.


Young Tae Kim

Secretary General, ITF

Kishan Vandael Schreurs


Researcher, VIAS Institute

Kishan Vandael Schreurs holds a Bachelor’s and Master's degree in Mobility Sciences - Traffic Safety. As a researcher he coordinates and performs fundamental and practical research, whilst providing policy advice. His fields of expertise are human behaviour, powered two wheelers, conflict and behavioural observation, (micro-)mobility, vehicle technology, and infrastructure. Furthermore, he has ready knowledge of fundamental road safety principles, data collection, and analyses (observations, self-composed questionnaires, experimental research, etc.). Lastly, he keeps continuing to develop expertise in the field of Powered Two Wheelers.
