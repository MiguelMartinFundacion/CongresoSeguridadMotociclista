Speakers





Raúl Aranda-Marco


Recognised researcher, LMU University of Munich

Dr. Raúl Aranda-Marco is a recognised researcher with solid expertise in the fields of impact biomechanics, human modeling, and crash simulation. He graduated in Mechanical Engineering from the University of Zaragoza and obtained his doctoral degree from Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München (LMU University of Munich) where he currently continues his research activity. Throughout his career, he has actively participated in various research projects related to motorcyclists' safety, specializing in the analysis of motorcyclists' airbag protectors.


Raúl Aranda-Marco

Recognised researcher, LMU University of Munich

Edwin Bastiaensen


Secretary General, IMMA

Edwin Bastiaensen is a Dutch national and holds Master of Science in Industrial Design Engineering at Delft University of Technology, The Netherlands.

He is an experienced leader in sustainable mobility and transport deployment. During his 25-year career, he held diverse global, regional, and national leadership positions in both industry and government oriented around R&D, intellectual property, regulatory affairs and business development.

In 2011 he was appointed as Secretary General of the International Motorcycle Manufacturers Association (IMMA), the voice of the global Motorcycle Industry towards institutions and regulatory forums, based in Geneva, Switzerland. IMMA currently celebrates its 75th anniversary.

Before IMMA, Edwin was Senior Manager Strategic Research/Engineering/IP with TomTom, the global leader in navigation technologies. He also provided consulting services for the European Motorcycle Industry Manufacturers Association (ACEM), coordinated EU-projects for ERTICO – ITS Europe with the automobile industry and provided consultancy services the European Commission on road infrastructure, Intelligent Transport Systems and Road Safety.

He started his career in the Ministry of Transport of the Netherlands researching and evaluating vehicle and mobility applications.


Edwin Bastiaensen

Secretary General, IMMA

Jesper Christensen


Director of the FIM Mobility Commission, FIM

Jesper Christensen, with a rich background in the IT industry since 1996, transitioned to a pivotal role in motorcycle road safety as the General Secretary of SMC in 2006. Under his leadership, SMC, boasting 70,000 members, has evolved into a modern organization focused on road safety, advanced rider training, and advocacy. Elected to the FIM's Public Affairs and later its Mobility Commission, Jesper has been instrumental in promoting motorcyclist rights, safety, and product quality on a global scale. His efforts extend to advising on international and EU laws affecting motorcycling, supporting FIM's national federations, and engaging with government agencies. A recognized figure in motorcycle road safety and an avid rider, Jesper's commitment to the community is unwavering.

Hennes Fischer


Senior advisor, Yamaha, Connected Motorcycle Consortium

Mr. Fischer's roots are firmly planted in the motoring industry. After study of mechanical engineering and an education in journalism, he first worked for a daily newspaper in Southern Germany and soon became editor of Germany’s "Motorrad" and "MO" motoring magazines. He joined Yamaha Motor Europe in the mid-1980s where he was actively involved in global product planning. He also worked as a research & development adviser for the famous Japanese bike manufacturer. Today he acts as a Senior Adviser to Yamaha, where he represents the company’s interests at ACEM (Association of European Motorcycle Manufacturers) at EU level in Brussels and in various consortia such as CMC (Connetcetd Motorcycle Consortium), SBMC (Swapping Batterie Motorcycle Consortium) C2C-CC (Car2Car Communication Consortium). He is actively involved in ITS developments and vehicle safety issues. With his combination of engineering background and journalist background, he bridges the technical and marketing disciplines in the motoring industries. He is a dedicated rider himself, with many years of road racing in both German national championship as well as European championship. His passion is documented by two motorcycle speed records (over distances of 100 and 1000 km) set in 1981 on the high-speed test track in Nardò, Italy. At a somewhat slower pace, Fischer still enjoys riding historical racing bikes and motorcycle touring onroad and offroad.


Hennes Fischer

Senior advisor, Yamaha, Connected Motorcycle Consortium

Jonas Jansson


Head of the department of road users, VTI

Dr. Jonas Jansson is head of the department Traffic and Road User at The Swedish National Road and Transport Research Institute (VTI). The Department focus on traffic safety, vehicle technology and human factors, as well as sustainable mobility and active modes of transportation. Dr. Jansson’s background is in driver support systems and vehicle dynamics. He was previously with Volvo Car Corporation between 1999 and 2007, where he worked as function owner for Volvos automatic emergency braking function. His thesis, in the area of automatic control, focus on pre-crash braking systems. Current areas of interest are the integration of road safety with sustainability mobility, remote operation of cooperative connected automated vehicles, electromobility and PTW safety.


Jonas Jansson

Head of the department of road users, VTI

Roberto Llamas


Road area manager, MITMA

ROADS, CHANNELS AND PORTS ENGINEER (ICCyP) and MASTER in TRAFFIC ENGINEERING from the Polytechnic University of Madrid.
More than 25 years of professional experience in ROADS in ROAD SAFETY, CONSERVATION, ROAD OPERATION.

He has held various positions of responsibility in the current Ministry of Transport and Sustainable Mobility (MTyMS), as Advisor to the Minister in infrastructures and safety and Safety Advisor in the former Ministry of Public Works, as well as Deputy Assistant Director-Coordinator of road safety in the DGC (General Directorate of Roads).

He currently holds the position of HEAD OF THE ROAD SAFETY UNIT in the DGC of the MTyMS, and is responsible for the road safety management system, specific road safety improvement programmes and the evaluation and classification of the safety of state roads in the DGC of the Ministry of Transport and Sustainable Mobility (accident rate analysis, accident concentration sections, sections with a high potential for safety improvement, guidelines for management procedures, road safety audits and inspections, evaluation and classification of the safety of the network -Network-wide Road Safety Assessment and ,...) and is the DIRECTOR AND TEACHER OF THE TRAINING PROGRAMMES of the State's ROAD SAFETY AUDITORS as well as responsible for the ACCREDITATION, CERTIFICATION AND REGISTRATION OF AUDITORS.

He has been in charge of the transposition of the European Directives on road infrastructure safety management (Directive 2008/96/EC and 1936/2019), implementation of their procedures, preparation of the guidelines for their application in Spain and their development and monitoring in the State Road Network (RCE).

He is also CHAIRMAN of the National Road Safety Committee of the ATC (Technical Road Association) and member of several International Road Safety Committees, such as the World Road Association (WRA/PIARC) and the Conference of European Directors of Roads (CEDR), as well as member of national experts in different Road Safety Committees of the European Commission (High Level Group on Road Safety -High Level Group on Road Safety-, Road Infrastructure Safety Management Committee, Road Safety Experts Committee, Road Safety Development Indicators Committee).


Roberto Llamas

Road area manager, MITMA

Antonio Perlot


ACEM Secretary General

An Italian-Swedish national, Antonio Perlot is the Secretary General of ACEM, the association representing the motorcycle industry in Europe. In this capacity since 2014, he is responsible for leading and coordinating the work of the association and representing the motorcycle industry at European and international levels. Prior to this, from 2006 to 2014, Mr Perlot served as ACEM Public Affairs Manager, advising the motorcycle industry on public and regulatory affairs.

Between 2001 and 2006, Mr Perlot worked for the Federation of European Motorcyclists’ Associations (FEMA), first as Campaigns Officer, then as Secretary General. Prior to that, he worked at EPRO as a public affairs consultant representing clients from different industry sectors. He has also worked in the NGO sector.

Mr Perlot holds a BA in Political Science (with a specialisation in European integration) from La Sapienza University and a MA in Diplomatic Studies from Società Italiana per l’Organizzazione Internazionale (SIOI).

Mª Carmen Plaza


Deputy Director for Road Safety and Road Sustainability, Community of Madrid

Civil engineer.

With more than 20 years dedicated to road safety and mobility and with a vocation for public service demonstrated by her extensive professional development in the Community of Madrid. She is Deputy Director General of Road Safety and Conservation in the General Directorate of Roads, of the Regional Ministry of Housing, Transport and Infrastructures of the Community of Madrid, and she also teaches in different masters as a specialist in road safety.

He began his professional career in road maintenance in a private company, to later dedicate himself exclusively to road safety and mobility tasks. His professional development has been mostly within the Administration, where he has promoted and implemented road safety management in a road network as complex as that of the Community of Madrid and is currently introducing a direct approach to Road Safety in the Conservation using digitisation and new technologies, such as Artificial Intelligence, to improve the management of the road network.

In recent years, it has been developing the necessary tools for the Community of Madrid to improve road safety in a more efficient way, taking into account not only the infrastructure but also the human factor and the vehicle, i.e. all the factors involved in accidents. It should be noted that he has promoted the creation of an Interdepartmental Road Safety Commission in the region and the drafting of a Road Safety Strategy, which for the first time will be multi-sectoral and cross-cutting in nature.

She is a prominent member of the Road Safety Committee of the Technical Association of Roads (ATC), where she has led several Working Groups throughout the almost 15 years of participation in this Association.

She is also Chair of the Road Safety Working Group of the Spanish Road Association's (AEC) Committee of Directors General of Autonomous Communities and Provincial Councils.


Mª Carmen Plaza

Deputy Director for Road Safety and Road Sustainability, Community of Madrid

Martin Winkelbauer


Expert for accident reconstruction, KFV

Martin Winkelbauer graduated in Mechanical Engineering at the Vienna Technical University in 1991. He joined KFV, the Austrian Road Safety Board, in 1993. In these more than 30 years, he performed research on various topics: driver education, testing and licensing, vehicle safety, infrastructure safety, cargo securing on trucks, child seats, driver assistance systems, efficiency assessment of road safety measures, etc. Motorcycle safety was a focus continuously, starting with research on ABS system, designing a test for moped riders and recently, implementing nudging elements for rider safety on rural roads. Since 2008, he also works as a sworn expert witness for accident reconstruction.
